# Hospital Policy Knowledge Base

Replace this file with your hospital's approved pre-operative and day-care discharge policies before using the assistant in a real setting.

## Fasting Instructions

Fasting advice depends on how soon the patient is from anesthesia, the type of intake being discussed, the procedure, and the hospital's written instructions. The assistant must never give a blanket yes or no about water, clear liquids, tea, coffee, food, or chewing gum without checking the timing first.

If the patient is asking about the day before surgery or many hours before the scheduled anesthesia time, that is not the same as the immediate pre-operative window. For many routine elective cases, plain water and other clear liquids may still be allowed until about 2 hours before anesthesia, while solids usually stop earlier. Written hospital instructions always override this general fallback, especially for high-risk patients or procedure-specific pathways.

If the exact surgery time is unclear, the instructions conflict, or the patient has not been told a fasting plan, the assistant should tell them not to guess and to call the hospital or anesthesiology team before changing what they eat or drink.

## Escort and Ride Home

For sedation, anesthesia, or day-care discharge, patients should arrange a responsible adult to accompany them home and stay reachable after discharge unless the treating hospital team has explicitly said otherwise. Patients should not assume they can drive themselves home after sedation or anesthesia. If the case is being done under local anesthesia alone and the team has given separate discharge instructions, those local instructions should be followed.

## Medications and Morning-of-Surgery Questions

Medication instructions can differ by patient and procedure. If the patient asks whether to continue or stop a medicine, the assistant should direct them to the hospital's pre-operative instructions or the treating team's advice instead of guessing.

## Arrival, Documents, and Contact Planning

Patients should keep their ID, hospital file, prior investigations, medication list, and an emergency contact ready. If the team has given an arrival time, they should follow that. If there is no arrival time or they have conflicting instructions, they should call the hospital.

## Escalation Rules

If the patient reports unclear instructions, conflict between written and verbal advice, urgent symptoms, or inability to arrange transport, the assistant should advise contacting the hospital or anesthesiology team directly.
